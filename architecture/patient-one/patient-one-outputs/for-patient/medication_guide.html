PARP Inhibitor + PI3K Inhibitor

These two medications work together to attack your cancer in different ways. Think of it like using two different keys to lock doors that cancer cells use to survive and grow.

Olaparib
Brand Name: Lynparza¬Æ

üî¨How It Works

Olaparib is a PARP inhibitor. Think of PARP as a repair tool that cancer cells use to fix their damaged DNA. Because you have a BRCA1 mutation, your cancer cells are already bad at repairing DNA. Olaparib blocks their last repair tool, causing the cancer cells to die.

Why it works for you: Your BRCA1 mutation makes your cancer especially vulnerable to this medication.

How to Take Two tablets (150mg each) twice daily
When to Take Morning and evening, about 12 hours apart
With or Without Food Can be taken with or without food
What It Looks Like Oval, green tablets marked with "OP150"

Alpelisib
Brand Name: Piqray¬Æ

üî¨How It Works

Alpelisib is a PI3K inhibitor. PI3K is like an "on switch" that tells cancer cells to grow and multiply. Your tests show this switch is stuck in the "on" position (PIK3CA mutation). Alpelisib turns off this switch, slowing or stopping cancer growth.

Why it works for you: Your tumor has high PIK3CA activity, making it a prime target for this drug.

How to Take One tablet (300mg) once daily
When to Take Same time each day with food
With or Without Food Must be taken WITH food
What It Looks Like Light pink, round tablets

  • Nausea - Take with food to minimize
  • Mouth sores - Special mouthwash can prevent/treat

  • Stay hydrated to prevent kidney problems

üí° Tips for Treatment Success

Managing Your Medications

  • Use a pill organizer - Helps track if you've taken your doses
  • Set phone alarms - Reminders for each dose time
  • Keep a medication diary - Track how you feel each day
  • Don't stop suddenly - Always talk to your doctor first

Managing Side Effects

  • For nausea: Eat small, frequent meals; try ginger tea
  • For fatigue: Balance activity with rest; light exercise can help
  • For rash: Use gentle, fragrance-free skincare; avoid hot water
  • For diarrhea: Follow BRAT diet (bananas, rice, applesauce, toast)
